Slide 85: Introduction to SVM

Instructions: Explain the goal of SVM: to find the optimal hyperplane.

Support Vector Machines (SVMs) are powerful supervised learning algorithms used for classification and regression. The primary goal of an SVM, especially in classification, is to find the optimal hyperplane that separates different classes of data points with the largest possible margin. This margin is the distance between the hyperplane and the nearest data points from each class, called support vectors. By maximizing the margin, SVM aims to achieve better generalization and robustness to unseen data.
