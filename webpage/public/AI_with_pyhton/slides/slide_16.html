Data Structures: Tuples

What are Tuples?

Tuples are ordered, immutable sequences of Python objects. They are similar to lists but with a key difference: Tuples cannot be modified after they are created.

Immutability

Immutability means that once a tuple is created, you cannot change its elements (you can't add, remove, or modify). This makes tuples safer to use when you want to ensure that data remains constant.

When to Use Tuples

  • When you want to ensure data integrity and prevent accidental modifications.
  • As keys in dictionaries (lists cannot be dictionary keys because they are mutable).
  • For returning multiple values from a function.
  • When performance is critical. Tuples are generally faster than lists due to their immutability.
  • Representing fixed collections of items (e.g., coordinates).
